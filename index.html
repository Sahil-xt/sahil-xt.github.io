Case studies

Three projects from my time at Mewurk — an all-in-one HRMS platform for Indian SMEs. Each shows a different design challenge: hardware UX, system-level thinking, and building trust on both sides of a product.

Case study 01 · Attendance & Kiosk
Face scan attendance — from mobile chaos to hardware kiosk
Redesigning how 500k+ employees clock in every day — and extending it to a dedicated face-recognition kiosk for factory and field settings.
Lead designerMobile + HardwareMewurk
The problem
HR managers at SMEs were spending hours reconciling attendance records. Employees were gaming location-based check-ins. Factory floors needed a touchless, zero-training solution for workers with low digital literacy.
My role
I led UX end-to-end — from early discovery with HR managers and field employees to shipped mobile flows and the kiosk interface. I worked closely with the PM and hardware engineering team throughout.
Key decisions
The kiosk UI had to work for workers with no smartphone experience. I stripped it down to a single action per screen, used large visual feedback states (green/red face frame), and replaced all text instructions with iconography and audio cues. On the admin side, I redesigned the dashboard to surface exceptions first — late arrivals, missed punches, anomalies — rather than raw logs.
What shipped
A face-scan kiosk experience requiring zero training, deployed across 1000+ SME customers. Mobile attendance with geo-fencing, selfie verification, and field visit tracking integrated into a single flow.
Case study 02 · Design system
Building Mewurk's design system from the ground up
As the product scaled to 15+ modules, inconsistency was slowing down design and engineering. I led the effort to identify patterns, document components, and ship a shared system.
Lead designerDesign systemsMewurk
The problem
With 15+ modules built over time by multiple hands, the product had accumulated UI debt — inconsistent spacing, duplicate components, no shared language between design and engineering. New features took longer to ship as teams reinvented patterns each time.
My role
I owned the design system initiative — auditing existing UI, identifying recurring patterns, defining component specs, and aligning with engineering on implementation. This ran alongside active product work, not as a separate project.
Key decisions
Rather than building a "perfect" system first, I took an incremental approach — documenting what existed, standardising the most-used patterns first (forms, tables, modals, notifications), and letting the system grow from real usage. I prioritised the admin-facing UI since that's where most complexity lived.
What shipped
A Figma component library covering core UI patterns, a pattern documentation wiki shared with engineering, and a naming convention adopted across all new modules. Reduced design-to-handoff time noticeably for new features.
Case study 03 · Payroll & Field visits
Making payroll transparent and field tracking non-intrusive
Two modules with the same core tension — powerful for admins, anxiety-inducing for employees. The challenge was building trust on both sides of the product.
Lead designerMobile + WebMewurk
The problem
Payroll errors are one of the top reasons employees distrust HR software — one wrong cycle destroys credibility. Field visit tracking felt surveillance-heavy to employees. Both modules needed to serve admin efficiency without alienating the people they affected most.
My role
I led design for both modules, running research sessions with HR managers and field employees separately to understand the trust gap. I worked closely with PM and engineering to define the right data boundaries for location tracking.
Key decisions
For payroll, I designed a pre-payroll review step that let HR managers catch issues before finalising — reducing errors at source. For employees, I added a payslip breakdown view explaining every deduction in plain language. For field visits, I advocated for employee-visible tracking — employees could see exactly what data was shared, which significantly reduced resistance to adoption.
What shipped
A payroll module with pre-run validation, employee-facing payslip breakdowns, and bulk correction flows for HR. A field visit module with transparent location sharing, visit history, and expense linking — used across 10+ industries.
