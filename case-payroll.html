Case 04 — Payroll

Payroll -- confidence before the lock

Exceptions-first dashboard + plain-language payslips + auto statutory tracking

RoleLead UX Designer
PlatformWeb + Mobile
UsersAccounts managers, HR admins, employees
Step 1 — Empathise

Payroll lock is the highest-stakes moment.

One wrong value affects every employee. Researched 30+ companies. The locked payroll is irreversible. HR anxiety peaks month-end.

💼
Accounts Manager
Verifies salary, taxes, transfers
Pain: Arrears calculation hard, investment proof validation tedious, statutory deadlines tricky
Need: Auto-flag anomalies, inline doc viewer, deadline countdown
👩‍💼
HR Manager
Runs payroll, handles queries
Pain: Month-end anxiety spike, too many employee questions, lock moment is terrifying
Need: Show anomalies only, impact preview before lock, plain-language payslips
👤
Employee
Receives salary, views payslip
Pain: Deductions confusing, no tax-saving guidance, can't dispute amounts
Need: Clear breakup visible, tax impact calculator, ability to raise queries

Insight from research: "Focus only on anomaly employees, not all 500" — an Accounts Manager quote that became the design principle. Show only exceptions. Make them resolvable inline. Then lock with confidence.

Step 2/3 — Define & Ideate

Exceptions-first: Show only what needs fixing

Tested three models: Run-first (too reactive), Checklist (doesn't surface real anomalies), Exceptions-first (chosen). In a 500-person company, typically 10-30 anomalies. Surface only those. HR resolves them. Then lock with confidence.

10-30
Anomaly employees out of 500
