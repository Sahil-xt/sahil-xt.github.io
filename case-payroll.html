Complex compliance, human consequences

Indian payroll involves a maze of statutory rules that most SME HR managers aren't equipped to navigate:

PFProvident Fund
ESIState Insurance
TDSTax Deducted at Source
PTProfessional Tax
LWFLabour Welfare Fund
Form 16TDS Certificate

"If we get payroll wrong even once, employees stop trusting us. It takes months to recover." — HR manager, manufacturing SME. Employees, meanwhile, got payslips full of accounting codes they didn't understand, making every deduction feel like an unexplained charge.

Define

Errors happen because HR can't see problems until it's too late

The core problem: HR only discovered attendance errors, pending leave requests, and overtime mismatches after starting the payroll cycle — when corrections meant re-runs. Employees received payslips with unexplained deductions. Four user roles (Admin, HR, Manager, Employee) each needed different access but were largely treated the same.

Design goal: Surface everything that could be wrong before the cycle locks permanently. Once exported, the file cannot be modified — so the pre-run step had to be thorough enough to catch everything.
Ideate

A pre-run gate before the point of no return

The key idea: a pre-run finalisation step that shows HR every reviewable item before export. Per employee: pending leave requests (approve/reject inline), attendance error count (with link to the log), approved overtime, and payable days. Only after HR has reviewed all of this does the Export button become the final action.

For employee payslips: every deduction in plain language, not accounting shorthand. "PF contribution (12% of basic)" not "PF: ₹2,400". Every line item explainable to a non-accountant.

Prototype

4 role states, 1 export flow

Prototyped the full pre-run flow in Figma with realistic data — anonymised records from actual Mewurk customers. Four distinct UI states based on role: Admin (full config), HR Manager (payroll run + export), Manager (team view only), Employee (own payslip). Payroll export was prototyped for compatibility with Tally, Zoho, and Saral Pay — each requiring different field mapping, which needed careful UX to avoid HR errors during export setup.

Test & Ship

The lock warning wasn't visible enough

HR managers were clicking through the export confirmation without fully reading the "this action is permanent" warning — it blended into the UI. We made the confirmation modal more explicit with a checkbox: "I confirm this payroll is final and cannot be changed after export." Payslip plain-language labels went through a second round of testing with a non-HR employee to verify every line was genuinely understandable.
