Case study 01 · Attendance & Kiosk

Face scan attendance — zero training on the factory floor

RoleLead UX Designer
PlatformWeb · Mobile · Hardware
Users500k+ employees, 1000+ SMEs
Empathise

Three users, three very different realities

HR managers spent 2–3 hours every Monday fixing last week's attendance errors — wrong locations, missed punches, employees marking themselves present from outside the office. Factory workers had never used a smartphone app. Office staff found geo-fencing unreliable near building entrances.

I ran discovery sessions across three user contexts: HR managers at desks, office employees during commute, and factory floor supervisors. Each had a completely different relationship with attendance tracking.

Key insight: Three contexts, three interface layers needed — admin reconciliation dashboard, mobile employee flow, and a hardware kiosk that needed zero onboarding.
Define

15+ status types, one coherent system

The attendance system handled 15+ status types — P, A, E, L, WO, P(H), A½ and more. Each had different correction rules and payroll implications. HR managers needed to surface anomalies fast; factory workers needed a sub-5-second clock-in with no decisions.

  • HR problem: Find and fix anomalies without wading through raw logs
  • Employee problem: Reliable clock-in that works without workarounds
  • Factory problem: Zero-training touchless attendance for low digital-literacy workers
Ideate

Single action per screen. Colour does the talking.

For the kiosk: stripped to one action per screen, large green/red face-frame feedback, audio confirmation replacing all text. Geo-fence (50m minimum) + selfie face recognition for mobile. Admin dashboard redesigned exceptions-first — anomalies and missed punches surfaced at the top, not buried in log tables.

For mobile reminders: rolling time picker (consistent with other modules), 10/20 min snooze, platform-native dismiss interactions. Default times: 8:50 AM clock-in, 6:10 PM clock-out.

Prototype

Tested on tablet, tested on real factory floors

Kiosk prototyped first as a Figma file on a tablet to simulate the hardware form factor. The face frame size was set at 60% screen height — large enough to guide positioning without any text instruction. The correction flow for admins covered all 15+ status types with shift-aware auto-filled times.

Test & Ship

Audio feedback was the unexpected hero

Factory floor testing revealed: workers looked away before seeing the green success frame. Silent confirmation caused them to stand and wait. Adding an audio beep resolved this completely. Battery saver mode on Android silently blocked reminders — we added an in-app prompt to disable it.
