Case study 02 · Leave & Holidays

Leave management — trust on both sides

RoleLead UX Designer
PlatformWeb · Mobile
ChallengePolicy complexity + employee trust
Empathise

The same system, completely different experiences

HR managers ran policy exceptions in Excel because the system was too rigid. Employees didn't know their leave balance until a request was rejected. Field employees discovered their comp-off had expired months after earning it — nobody told them there was an expiry.

I ran separate research sessions with HR managers and employees — because their experience of the same system was so different that a shared session would have skewed the findings.

Core tension: HR needed maximum configurability. Employees needed maximum transparency. More policy options = more complexity to explain to employees. Designing for both without sacrificing either was the central challenge.
Define

Two products hiding inside one module

Indian SME leave policies are genuinely complex: annual, sick, casual, comp-off, short leave (at start/end/break of day), optional holidays requiring approval, carry-forward limits, accrual rules, encashment, and state-specific regional holidays by office location.

  • For HR: Policy builder flexible enough to handle all scenarios — without needing Excel for exceptions
  • For employees: Understand entitlements and constraints before applying — not as rejection errors after
Ideate

Progressive disclosure + proactive transparency

Admin side: Policy builder with sensible defaults upfront, advanced rules (accrual, carry-forward, encashment) revealed on an "advanced settings" toggle. Works for new HR managers and experienced ones equally.

Employee side: Leave accrual summary showing every grant and deduction in plain language ("added", "expired", "rolled over" — not "credited", "lapsed", "carried forward"). Team availability calendar inside the apply flow. Inline policy constraints visible before submit, not as errors after.

Prototype

Parallel prototyping — admin and employee together

I prototyped both sides simultaneously, because every admin configuration decision changed what the employee saw. Testing them in isolation would have missed the connection. The comp-off expiry warning (at 30 days) was added after a prototype session where employees consistently missed the expiry date.

Test & Ship

Plain language fixed the biggest confusion

First round of testing: employees still didn't understand the accrual summary even when it was visible. The language was the problem — "credited" and "lapsed" were HR terms, not everyday words. Rewriting with a non-HR employee to verify each label resolved this. Optional holidays were also merged into the main apply flow after employees looked for them in the wrong place.
